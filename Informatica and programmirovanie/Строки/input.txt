The remnants of Napoleon's army and the emperor himself crossed the Berezina River on 23.11.1812. Napoleon, ahead of his own regiments, fled Russia. On 25 December 1812, Alexander 1 issued a manifesto on the end of the war. Now the Russian army had to fight outside its homeland. In 1813, she occupied Warsaw and Berlin. An anti-French coalition was formed, which included Austria, England, Prussia and Russia. The combined forces of the Allies on 10.04.1813 managed to inflict a crushing defeat on Napoleon in the Battle of the Peoples near Leipzig. On 31 March 1814, the Russian army entered Paris. Napoleon abdicated and was exiled to the island of Elba.
